Introduction

A Traverse is a chain of straight lines to be used as a basis for the measurement of details. It is the field operation of measuring the lengths and directions of a series of straight lines connecting a series of points on the earth. Each of these straight lines is called a traverse leg, and each point is called a traverse station.

The principle of traversing is that if the direction and length of any line is known then the coordinates of its head (end point) can be found from the coordinates of its tail (starting point).

The types of traverse are:

  1. Closed Link Traverse

Type of Traverse

a) Closed Linked Traverse
It starts either from a known station and ends at the same station or another known station. Closed traverses have more checks and are accurate, as they provide both line and angular checks. There are two kinds of closed traverses:

  1. Closed Loop Traverse
  2. Link Traverse

b) Open Linked Traverse
It either starts from a known or unknown station and ends at another unknown or known station. It is geometrically and mathematically open. Checks are only possible by additional observation. It has no checks itself and is used in linear alignment, suitable for long, narrow strips.

Purpose of Traversing

The purpose of traversing is based on the basic principle of surveying, i.e., working from whole to parts. It is carried out for the following purposes:

  • To establish control points for chain, compass, PT, topographical, cadastral, and photogrammetric surveys.
  • To establish control points for calculating earthwork quantities.
  • To fix the alignment for different engineering projects when better accuracy is required and for locating control points for railroads, highways, and other construction work.
  • To ascertain the coordinates of boundary pillars.
  • To determine existing boundary lines.
